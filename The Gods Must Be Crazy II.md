**EXT. KALAHARI - VARIOUS - DAY**

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
They have lived undisturbed for 20,000 years because they are the only people in the world who know how to live without surface water. When ivory poachers in high-tech vehicles intrude into this vast thirst-land to hunt elephant, they have to drag giant reservoirs of water with them, and when the water runs low, they have to hightail it out of the Kalahari again... so the Bushmen work and play in peaceful isolation, quite unaware of the crowded and hectic world outside their domain, and even of the wars that are being fought on the very fringes of the Kalahari.

Sometimes Xixo tells about the time when he went to look for the end of the Earth, and about the strange heavy people he met on his journey, but he finds it difficult to describe the beings who live outside the Kalahari, and he always ends up by saying that the heavy people are persons who seem to know some magic that can make things move, and even fly, but that they're not very bright, because they can't survive without their magic contrivances.

In the mornings, they like to read the news. They can read that the hyena has a new girlfriend, that the cheetah has lost one of her babies, and the oryx is starting to migrate to the west. The older children teach the younger ones how to read all the gossip about their neighbors, the animals, because everything that happens in the Kalahari gets printed out in the sand.
